PD EarlyScreen Kit
Non-Invasive · High Accuracy · Low Cost

Technologically Advanced, Clinically Validated

Our PD EarlyScreen Kit utilizes peripheral blood methylation analysis technology, detecting 10 key methylation sites related to Parkinson's pathology, combined with our proprietary AI algorithm model to generate accurate PD risk scores.

The technology has been validated in a multicenter retrospective study involving 1,889 samples, achieving an AUC of 0.73, significantly better than early clinical diagnosis accuracy (approximately 53%).

Completely Non-Invasive

Only requires 5ml of peripheral blood, no lumbar puncture or radioactive scanning needed, with patient acceptance rates exceeding 95%.

Significant Cost Benefits

Testing costs only 8% of PET scans and 15% of CSF tests, making large-scale population screening feasible.

Fast and Efficient

From sample collection to result output takes only 3-5 working days, supporting efficient workflow for medical institutions.

Our Innovation Story

"When my grandmother suffered from missed early intervention opportunities for Parkinson's disease, I was determined to change this situation."

The technical lead Wenhao Wu's grandmother was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease in 2018, when symptoms were already quite noticeable, missing the optimal intervention window. This family experience made us deeply aware of the importance of early screening and also revealed the significant limitations of existing diagnostic methods.

Meanwhile, our research found that China has over 3 million Parkinson's patients, and this number continues to grow rapidly with an aging population. However, existing early diagnosis methods are either highly invasive (such as cerebrospinal fluid testing with refusal rates exceeding 70%) or prohibitively expensive (such as PET scans costing over $1,500 per session), unable to meet large-scale screening needs.

From Personal Pain Point to Societal Solution

Based on cutting-edge neuroepigenetics research, we discovered the strong correlation between peripheral blood DNA methylation and Parkinson's disease. After over two years of research, we screened 10 most predictive methylation sites from thousands of candidate markers, constructing the first Parkinson's early screening model suitable for the Chinese population.

Our vision is to make Parkinson's early screening as common as blood pressure testing, becoming part of routine physical examinations for middle-aged and elderly individuals, advancing diagnosis time by an average of 2 years, significantly improving patients' quality of life and prognosis.
